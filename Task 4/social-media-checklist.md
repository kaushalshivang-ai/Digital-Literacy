# Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist

## 5 Do's for Responsible Social Media Use
1. **Do review your privacy settings regularly:** Ensure your personal accounts are set to private and strictly control who can view your posts, photos, and personal information.
2. **Do think before you post:** Always assume that future employers, internship coordinators, or professors might see your content. Keep your public-facing footprint professional.
3. **Do verify information before sharing:** Combat the spread of misinformation by double-checking the source of news or viral posts before sharing them with your network.
4. **Do use social media for positive networking:** Engage thoughtfully with industry leaders, college alumni, and academic groups on platforms like LinkedIn or Twitter.
5. **Do ask for permission before posting others:** Always get consent from your friends, batchmates, or faculty members before posting photos or videos of them online.

## 5 Don'ts for Responsible Social Media Use
1. **Don't share sensitive personal details:** Never post your phone number, home address, real-time location, college ID card, or financial information publicly.
2. **Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling:** Avoid using offensive language, harassing others, or participating in toxic online arguments. 
3. **Don't post complaints about your college or peers:** Venting about professors, coursework, or classmates on public platforms is unprofessional and can lead to disciplinary action.
4. **Don't accept requests from strangers:** Be wary of unknown profiles, as they could be bots, scammers, or bad actors attempting to harvest your personal data.
5. **Don't rely on a single, weak password:** Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms, and don't skip setting up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for your accounts.
